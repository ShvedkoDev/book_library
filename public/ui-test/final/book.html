The Sun Also Rises

A Classic Novel

by Ernest Hemingway
First published 1926 • 251 pages • English • Fiction

Hemingway's profile of the Lost Generation reflects his own experiences in the post-WWI world and during the liberation of Paris, portraying both nihilistic resignation and authentic determination.

This educational edition has been adapted for Micronesian students with additional cultural context and discussion questions that connect universal themes to local experiences and values.

  • Overview
  • Details
  • Reviews
  • Other Versions

Set in 1920s Paris and Spain, this novel follows Jake Barnes, an American journalist, and his friends as they navigate post-war disillusionment. The story explores themes of love, loss, masculinity, and the search for meaning in a world changed by war.

This edition includes supplementary materials designed for Pacific Islander students, with cultural parallels and discussion guides that help connect the universal themes of the Lost Generation to contemporary Micronesian experiences.
